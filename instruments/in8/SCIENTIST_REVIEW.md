# IN8 Scientist Review

Please write comments anywhere in this file. Short notes are ideal; there is
no form, syntax, or complete answer expected. TAVI maintainers will reconcile
your comments with the references and make any code changes.

The five questions that would help most are:

1. What are the real virtual-source width and height, and is an approved H10
   source spectrum available?
2. What are the ThermES analyser slab count, slab size, mosaic, and actual
   vertical focusing behavior as a function of final wavevector?
3. Where are the alpha-2, alpha-3, and alpha-4 collimators and slits, and what
   are their lengths and clear apertures? Is there definitely no alpha-1 slot?
4. What are the current monochromator/analyser mechanical bending limits and
   the correct take-off-angle envelope?
5. Are there one or two PG filters in the ThermES path, where are they, and
   how are PG and Be filter modes inserted or removed?

## What TAVI currently assumes

The model uses arm lengths 2.28 / 2.48 / 1.05 / 0.70 m and scattering senses
+1 / +1 / -1. Those signs were checked against a live vTAS run. PG(002)
monochromator geometry is based on the available papers; much of the analyser,
collimator, slit, Cu(200), filter, and source detail remains provisional.

FlatCone and IMPS are deliberately deferred rather than treated as missing
single-detector details.
